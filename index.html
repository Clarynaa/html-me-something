Clary Green's Programming Journey

First steps

Clary first began programming around 2002, back in the days of Myspace.♦
Profile editing was all the rage back then, and she delved a little into the HTML, CSS, and Javascript that made up a page.



A Long Break, An Ambitious Goal

Years later, sometime in the early 2010's Clary and her partner decided that they had a dream they wanted to bring to life.
That dream was a game. Upon doing research they decided upon Hero Engine.♠
It was an ambitious project that even with paid professionals helping dive into the API for Hero Engine there was almost no progress.



Take Two and Three

A short hiatus and the two of them decided to continue pursuing their dream in a different game engine, Unity.♥
Going through programming classes at the same time as working on the project, Clary had a bit more of a handle on things this time
She managed to get a few basics working, but then hit a wall, and gave up.

A few months later, Clary finished her programming course and dove back into Unity.
With determination she managed to make a few "games" by following tutorials. She felt ready for a real game.
Clary managed to create a working prototype of a single round of all of the mechanics that would be present in the game.
Something in her AI code broke most of her code. Nothing worked right anymore. She was completely demoralized and gave up again.



The State of Things

After a few more years Clary wanted to pursue her game again. This time she applied to some scholarships while self-teaching.
Upon receiving a scholarship from Google and Udacity to learn android programming she got the drive to code for a living.
Clary abandoned the idea of working on her game until she could program better, but instead threw herself head first into the scholarship.
After the first unit of the android scholarship finished she had received word that she was accepted to LaunchCode as well.
Android wasn't Clary's thing, and she didn't have time to work on both, so she accepted the LaunchCode scholarship and left Android behind.
Her journey rapidly grew in pace, and she has become a mentor as well as receiving praise from many professionals and talent acquisition teams.
She is currently well on her way towards the new dream of being a professional software developer.

Notes:
♦ Myspace was a social media website that was popular in the early 2000's, before facebook.
♠ Hero Engine is a Massively Multiplayer game engine for developing your own multiplayer games.
